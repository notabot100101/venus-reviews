# Body-Safe Materials Guide

Understanding what materials touch your body is essential for safe, enjoyable use. This guide covers why material safety matters, what to choose, what to avoid, and how to verify product claims.

*Updated July 22, 2026 with clearer material verification and shopping-safety notes.*

## Why Materials Matter

Skin contact products come into direct touch with sensitive mucous membranes and breakable skin. The right materials:

- **Prevent allergic reactions** - Porous materials trap bacteria and cause rashes
- **Protect mucous membranes** - Body-safe plastics and silicone are gentle on sensitive tissue
- **Reduce infection risk** - Non-porous surfaces resist bacterial growth
- **Prevent chemical exposure** - Medical-grade materials avoid harmful phthalates and BPA

Some materials contain phthalates, which are plasticizers added to make plastics more flexible. Phthalates can migrate during use and exposure, potentially causing hormonal disruptions. Look for materials that have been medically tested.

## Safe Materials

### Medical-Grade Silicone

The gold standard for intimate products. Medical-grade silicone is:

- **Non-porous** - Doesn't harbor bacteria or mold
- **Body-safe** - Tested to meet medical device standards
- **Durable** - Won't degrade with cleaning
- **Pleasant texture** - Soft but smooth against skin
- **Temperature resistant** - Can be safely warmed for use

**How to verify:** Look for "medical-grade silicone" or "100% body-safe silicone" in product descriptions. Some products display ISO 10993 certification marks.

### ABS Plastic

High-quality ABS plastic is:

- **Dense and durable** - Won't bend or warp easily
- **Easy to clean** - Non-porous surface
- **Transparent options available** - You can see through to verify no debris inside
- **Cost-effective** - Good for entry-level products

**Caution:** Not all plastics are equal. Avoid cheap PVC or polyvinyl chloride. Look for dense, smooth plastic with no visible cracks or bubbles.

### Stainless Steel

Food-grade stainless steel (316/L607) is:

- **Hypoallergenic** - Won't cause reactions
- **Temperature conductive** - Can be warmed for sensation
- **Incredibly durable** - Lasts years, not months
- **Easy to sterilize** - Hospital-grade cleaning

### Glass

Borosilicate glass is:

- **Non-porous** - Perfect for thorough cleaning
- **Temperature conductive** - Safe for warm use
- **Aesthetically pleasing** - Transparent designs
- **Easy to clean** - No pores trap bacteria

### Wood

Only use properly sealed, body-safe woods:

- **Oiled hardwood** - Teak, maple, with food-grade mineral oil
- **Never use** - Exotic woods, untreated timber
- **Always verify** - Non-toxic sealant on surface

## Unsafe Materials

### Porous Materials (Avoid)

**Jelly, rubber, TPE, some PVC:**

- **Trap bacteria** - Pores harbor microbes, can't be fully cleaned
- **Leach chemicals** - Phthalates migrate over time
- **Degrade** - Cracks and tears form quickly
- **Allergy risk** - Unknown formulations can cause reactions

### Unknown Composites

**Products that don't disclose materials:**

- **Risk unknown** - Could be anything mixed in
- **No accountability** - Can't verify safety claims
- **Hidden hazards** - Phthalates, BPA, etc.

## How to Verify Claims

### Check Product Listings

- **Material listed?** Medical-grade silicone or similar
- **Certification marks?** ISO 10993, FDA cleared
- **Brand reputation?** Known for safety commitments
- **Transparent?** Full disclosure of materials

### Read Reviews Carefully

- **Report material issues?** Users note reactions
- **Long-term durability?** Comments on degradation
- **Cleaning reports?** Feedback on hygiene
- **Safety concerns?** Reviews flag problems

### When in Doubt

**Choose:**

- Medical-grade silicone
- Body-safe ABS plastic
- Glass or stainless steel
- Properly sealed wood

**Avoid:**

- Jelly/rubber toys
- Products with unlisted materials
- Extremely cheap options
- Items without safety certifications

## Summary

**Best choices:** Medical-grade silicone, glass, stainless steel, ABS plastic

**Avoid:** Porous materials (jelly, rubber), unknown composites, unlisted materials

**Verify:** Look for certification marks, transparent material disclosure, reputable brands
